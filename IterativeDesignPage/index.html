Iterative Design

Context

From wireframing to low-fidelity prototypes to high-fidelity prototypes, this group project designed an interactive interface over Figma for a startup called "Brandazine". The main objective was to create a prototype of an application for the startup. Multiple iterations of peer reviews and online user testings were conducted to create well-designed application that is easy to navigate.

Startup Information


Inedit (Brandazine) is a fashion retail service for content creators. It is an exclusive fashion rental service for creators on Instagram and Youtube. Creators pay a monthly subscription to gain unlimited access to fashion products and the opportunity to sell their content to brands. They were founded in 2020 and are based in South Korea with a team size of 10. The company is co-founded by Matt and Keon, who’ve both been in the creator space for over 4 years, as well as Ikhan, whose co-founded a fashion rental company previously. Brandazine has grown to $80K MRR with 100 brands and 500 creators and have stayed profitable for the past 5 months through positive unit economics. The users of this application include content creators and the fashion brands. Based on a conversation with the co-founder, Keon, there seems to be a content creator side of the product for mobile, but no mobile product for the brand side yet. Critique

Below are the feedbacks and comments we received during the Studio critique.

    Accessibility
  • Some fonts, especially the header fonts are difficult to read.
  • cons in the bottom navigation bar are intuitive for the most part, but text under would be beneficial.
  • Provide more information about what the check marks mean on the profile page.
  • For the subscriptions, provide more distinct color contrast with respect to the plan’s ranking. For example, the gold should not stand out more when platinum is the best plan.

    Navigational
  • Can’t return to landing screen if one accidentally clicks on content creator instead of fashion brand.
    Functionality
  • Suggestion to implement a free trial to allow users to try the service before committing to a subscription.

User Testing


Introduction (What we wrote on user testing):

Imagine you are a fashion content creator and want to connect easily with fashion brands to partner with them and rent their clothing for your videos. **NOTE: This is not an actual website, this is an interactive mockup made on prototyping software. In order to navigate the prototype, use your mouse to click on the iPhone screen. Blue highlighted sections will appear indicating where you can click to navigate to another screen. Make sure to think aloud as you perform the tasks so we better understand your thought process when navigating the site.

Testing Instructions

1. Without leaving the homepage, what are your initial impressions of the website? Explain your answer.

2. Sign up as a Content Creator. [Success: Yes, No]

3. Locate recommendations. [Success: Yes, No]

4. Locate brands. [Success: Yes, No]

5. Locate the brands you are partnered with. [Success: Yes, No]

6. View the items inside your cart. [Success: Yes, No]

7. We are going to switch it up now. Press "r" to return to the initial screen. Imagine you are now a high-end fashion brand looking for creators to partner with to promote your fashion. [Success: Yes, No]

8. Continue to the fashion brand profile. [Success: Yes, No]

9. Locate trending content. [Success: Yes, No]

10. Locate partner creators. [Success: Yes, No]

11. Find recommended creators. [Success: Yes, No]

12. Find the clothing that is currently in stock. [Success: Yes, No]

13. View your team members. [Success: Yes, No]

14. Do you feel confident conducting business on this website, either as a fashion brand or a content creator? Explain your answer.

15. Do you know any companies or websites that are similar to this one? If so, explain how this one compares.

Questions We Asked

1. What frustrated you most about this site?

2. If you had a magic wand, how would you improve this site?

3. What did you like about the site?

4. How likely are you to recommend this site to a friend or colleague (0=Not at all likely, and 10=Very likely)?

User Testing Summary & Analysis

Task Summary

For the user testing portion of this endeavor, users were tasked with figuring out how to navigate the app first as a content creator looking to rent high-end fashion, and then as a brand looking to recruit content creators. The subtasks asked users to explain their thought process while finding the main features of the app, including finding recommended clothing to rent as a content creator, and where to manage your stock as a brand.

User Performance

Generally, users were easily able to complete all tasks, and gave positive feedback about the layout of the app. However, some users had difficulty figuring out which page certain features of our app were on, including partner brands, recommended creators, and stock. Some users were also occasionally confused by the wording of the headings, for instance confusing the “Creator Content” section for the “Partner Creators” section, or confusing “Brands” on the homepage for “Partner Brands” on the businesses page.

Future Changes

If we were to continue to iterate on this interface we would spend time redesigning the layout of the headings to ensure they are as intuitive as possible. We also received feedback that our color scheme and font could be improved to exude confidence and sophistication since we are a high-end fashion rental service, so we would also continue to improve our aesthetic.
